Emotional Intelligence

April 22, 2021 by Ryan

An investigation and write up into what emotional intelligence is as I understand it.

IQ vs EQ

IQ is a well known, although flawed measure of intelligence. It's well known and the term is often used, however the meaning of the abbreviation is not. EQ is even less known, so what do they actually mean?

  • IQ is an abbreviation for “Intelligence Quotient” and is supposed to be a measure of someone's "relative intelligence".
  • EQ, likewise, is "Emotional Quotient" (sometimes referred to as EI) and is someone's ability to identify and manage their own or another's emotions.

According to some online IQ test, I have an IQ of "125 or higher" what this really means is absolutely nothing no one should take these seriously.

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence (EQ) is stated by Daniel Goleman as containing 5 distinct elements:

  1. Self-awareness
  2. Self-regulation
  3. Motivation
  4. Empathy
  5. Social Skills

Breaking these down further, we can see how each skills affects our overall EQ:

  • Self-awareness is one's own ability to identify and acknowledge one's own feelings and emotions and understand how these emotions and actions can affect those around. You understand your own strengths and weaknesses and how to be humble.
  • Self-regulation is to have control over one's own actions. Having good self-regulation means one is not likely to lash out, make poorly influenced decisions, compromise values. Those with strong self-regulation are able to stay in control.
  • Motivation is important to consistent work towards fulfilling goals. To achieve to a high standard, it's required to stay highly motivated.
  • Empathy helps someone understand the way another feels, how they are motivated, anything else that may be happening. Empathy can tie in a lot to self-awareness & self-regulation. Those with strong empathy skills can understand someone through body-language and their responses.
  • Social skills can be a very important tool in working with others. Conflict resolution is an important skill needed when working with others and is directly influenced by communication skills. Social skills also include understanding queues, giving and receiving feedback or praise and being approachable.

Why EQ is so important now

While EQ was not a term coined until 1990, it's a set of skills practiced since people began working in groups. By now you should have a good understanding of why EQ is important in perceiving, processing and regulating emotional information, but why are these skills important?

Understanding and practicing good EQ provides a framework for interaction in which we make them more rewarding, supportive and healthy for everyone involved. We are not solitary beings, whether you like it or not, you are required to interact with others in various settings and practicing good EQ can improve these situations for you and the other participants.
