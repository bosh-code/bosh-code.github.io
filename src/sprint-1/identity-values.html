An Ethical Decision

While working for an MSP in Auckland, I was constantly facing decisions around "doing the right thing". Labor costs are not cheap and as a contractor for clients, my time could be a huge bill. Clients would often want temporary fixes or the cheapest solution possible, which often meant I would need to be back again soon when the wheels inevitably fell off. Often times I really felt for the clients, IT is was not something they knew a lot about, just that it was expensive and a pain when it didn't work.
Some clients were really kind and I knew their financial situations weren't great, so when they requested stop-gap solutions to issues, I tried to do the right thing for them. I would spend un-billable time for them after-hours, either on-site or remotely when it was needed. They wouldn't be able to pay for the time anyway and everyone needs IT to do their jobs, so it always seemed the ethical thing to do.
