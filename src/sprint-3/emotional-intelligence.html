Workload Management

 Self management will be important for me going forwards. Throughout uni, most of my time was self managed and initially I struggled with it. However I learnt to cope with the workload by using a few key skills, such as;

  • Smart goals or "smarter goals" helped me to break tasks down into smaller chunks that coule be completed in a clear, individual and time-based way. Smarter goals must contain the following criteria.
    • Specific
    • Measurable
    • Achievable
    • Relevant
    • Time bound
    • Evaluated
    • Reviewed
  • Time-boxing was used with Smart goals in the time-bound criterion. I often found myself using "hard time boxing".
  • I also found myself often using a combination of mostly 45-15 work to break boxes and 15/20-5 depending on the task

Scheduling

 Usually I take a looser approach to scheduling and I find it works best for me. Days are scheduled for certain smart goals, based on work that needs to be done. I don't rigorously plan out a days activities ahead of time, just the smart goal or goals for the day so I have an idea of my desired achievable(s). During the day, at the start of each goal, I will box out times based on how long I think I need. I find this method of planning to be similar to agile in it's cycle of planning, doing then reviewing and it helps me to stay on task and focused.
I try to work around my partner's work times, so we have time together in the evenings, also I usually spend a couple of hours each day on the weekend working.

Help
