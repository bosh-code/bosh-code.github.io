Neuroplasticity, growth mindset & their influence on learning

Understanding

Neuroplasticity is a general term for some complex things that happen in the brain. It's the ability of our neural networks to change, like neural pathways making new connections. For example, neuroplasticity changes can occur from some of the following:

  • Learning new things and abilities
  • Environmental and external influences
  • Practicing a skill or task
  • Stress

Our brains remain "plastic" well into adulthood, meaning our ability to learn and practice is not lost as a child. A lot of research has been conducted into what learning is and how we learn, as well as ways to maximise our neuroplasticity. You can read more about that here.

The growth mindset

A growth mindset is the opposite of a fixed mindset. In a fixed mindset, we believe that our character, intelligence and creative abilities are fixed or static, and that we are unable to change them in a meaningful way. Success is therefore confirming our bias that intelligence is inherent, which can lead to an avoidance if risk taking and failure.

Practicing a growth mindset creates a passion for learning, confidence to take risks and understanding that failure is not a negative experience, it is learning and another step towards achievement.

The characteristics of a growth mindset can be summed up in a few key points:

  • A desire to learn
  • Persistence during setbacks
  • Knowing mastery comes from effort
  • Learning from criticism
  • Learning and being inspired by others

An example of growth vs fixed mindset is children completing a jigsaw puzzle. Children who prefer to repeat previously completed "easy" puzzles, are practicing a fixed mindset, whereas those who chose a new more difficult puzzle were of a growth mindset. Their definition of success was different, those with the fixed was just about completing puzzles, however, those with growth, were about learning.

The influence on learning

To increase neuroplasticity for a positive effect on learning, I'm trying to break up some of my daily routine, introducing novel and different tasks to establish new pathways. I'm also trying to get more exercise into my day, but that can be a challenge.

To practice a good growth mindset and incorporate it into my learning plan, I'm taking more time during my reflections, thinking about what was challenging during my sprint and why I found it challenging.
